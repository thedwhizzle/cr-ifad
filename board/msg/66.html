THE GOLF CLUB OF CHAPEL RIDGE
IFAD MEN’S GROUP
RULES OF PLAY


Season: March 1st through October 31st

Days of Play: Monday – Wednesday – Friday


IFAD MEMBERSHIP REQUIREMENTS:

• Member of Chapel Ridge Golf Club
• Evidence of an Established USGA Handicap
• Payment of Non-refundable $25 Annual Membership Fee
• Payment of $1 Dues Every Day of Play
• Follow USGA Rules of Golf
• Recording of All Scores (Home and Away) in a USGA approved handicap system consistent with Equitable Stroke Control
• Protecting the Field is Every Player’s Duty & Responsibility
• Participate in Annual Rules & Handicap Seminar each February


NEW IFAD MEMBER CANDIDATES:

In addition to the foregoing, candidates for membership shall either play their first round or have played a round with a member of the IFAD Committee for the purposes of general orientation and handicap verification


TEE TIMES:

Best efforts will be made to procure five (5) tee times for each day of play. Members will be responsible for communicating via email to the general IFAD mailbox – IFAD.GOLFER@GMAIL.COM – their desire to play on any given day at least two days before the day of play. This will allow the Committee to notify the pro shop the status of tee times the day prior to play. The pro shop will not be responsible for taking individual member’s reservations. Reservations will be first come, first served as noted by time of g-mailbox delivery. When there are more than two (2) groups a shotgun start will be in place using the 18th hole and when five groups are present the 17th hole.

GENERAL RULES OF PLAY:

• First day of play is Friday, March 2
• Last day of play is Wednesday, October 31
• Annual $25 Membership Fee is due by February 1
• USGA Rules of Play will be followed
• Daily rules of play may be altered by the Committee, or it’s designate to respond to course conditions
• When conditions are CART PATH ONLY, play will be LIFT, CLEAN, and PLACE, otherwise the ball is played down
• The ball must be holed out, unless the player has picked up due to reaching the hole maximum
• Actual Gross Scores must be recorded on the official scorecard
• A maximum score of NET Triple Bogey is allowed, after reaching which the player must pick up
• Guests will not be allowed to play in the group wager. However, if room is available within the tee-times provided guests may play along with a member.
• Speed of play is defined as 2 Hours and 15 Minutes per nine holes with ten minutes allotted for the turn between nines; The Committee shall address individual speed of play concerns
• Ready Golf is the norm. Ready Golf is defined as being immediately behind the group in front of your group, not as being immediately ahead of the group behind your group; Ready Golfers should be at their ball in advance of their turn of play, have their club selected, play out of turn in order to speed up play if your playing partners are not ready and read your putt in advance of your turn if it doesn’t interfere with others.
• All play shall be from the same set of tees. The Committee may set play from the Blue, White or Gold tees on any given day
• To be included in play on a given day, the member must be present 15 minutes before the first tee-time to allow for team formation, etc.
• The Committee may alter rules of play throughout the year as it deems necessary to maximize the enjoyment of the competition
• Repeated violations of Rules of Play will be addressed by the Committee and may result in expulsion from the group



WAGERING:


• The daily group wager shall be $5 per man
• Individual wagers may be negotiated before play commences, and presses are allowed, but are not mandatory
• The nature of the daily wager shall be the responsibility of the Committee or it’s designate
• Different forms of competition may incorporate handicap adjustments in accordance with USGA Recommendations
• Once play has commenced, both the daily wager and the IFAD dues must be paid by the member regardless of whether the member finishes the round
• Team stroke play events shall be formed when equal number of players are available for play (2-, 3- and 4-man teams); when there are three or less teams, winning team takes all; when there are four or more teams the split will be 60% to 1st place and 40% to 2nd place
• Individual stroke play events shall be formed when there are an uneven number of players (e.g. 5,7,11,13,17); The Wager shall be $3 for the skin pool and $2 for the low net pool; Skins play shall use 50% of handicap with NO strokes on par three’s. Handicaps shall be rounded down to an even number (e.g. if a player’s handicap is 7 that player shall get three shots total in the skins game and 7 shots total in the low net competition).The low net shall pay out 60% to 1st place and 40% to 2nd place or 1st place ties split the pot
• Two-man match play or partner match play events may be formed when there are an even number of players (e.g. 4, 10, 14, 18)
• Foursome selection in team or individual stroke play will be A-B-C-D to encourage speed of play
• Foursome and player selection in match play will be through random ball toss; the intent for two-man match play is that everyone play a match against every other player by year’s end so it may end up being less than random at some point
• The Committee has targeted one day per week to be individual stroke play, and one day every other week to be two-man match play; the remainder of play days will be some form of team competition

COMPETITION SCORING:

• A point system will be employed to determine the competition’s outcome
• Team stroke play: members of winning team receive 3 points each; when there are four or more teams competing, second place team members receive 1 point each
• Individual stroke play: 1st Place – 5 points; 2nd Place – 3 points; 3rd Place – 1 point
• Individual match play winner receives 3 points; in the event of a draw each player receives 1.5 points
• Partner match play winners receive 2 points each; when there are five or more teams second place winners receive 1 point each
• Winners for the year shall be awarded based upon a per round weighting of points won per round played. To qualify, a member must play a minimum of 35 rounds during the season. This will allow members who don’t play every day to be in the running for the annual competition.


USE OF $1 IFAD POOL:

In addition to the annual membership fee, each member shall contribute $1 to the IFAD Pool every day that member plays. These funds shall be used to cover the expenses associated with the annual party, expenses for trophies or other types of recognition as deemed appropriate by the Committee, and to create a fund for the annual tip for the pro shop staff.
